\vspace*{0.5cm}
Let $\by$ be an $n \times 1$ vector, $\bX=\mathtt{1}_{n}$ be an $n \times 1$ vector of ones, $\bbeta$ is a scalar, and $\beps$ is $n \times 1$ vector of errors.  We write a regression equation in the vector form as follows
\begin{align*}
\by=\bX\beta+\beps.
\end{align*}
\begin{enumerate}
\item{Write down $\bX'\bX$? What are the numbers of rows and columns?}
\item{Is $\bX'\bX$ invertible?}
\item{Write down $\bX'\by$.What are the numbers of rows and columns?}
\item{What are the numbers of rows and columns of $\bX'\by$?}
\item{Suppose that the above variables and equation satisfy the Gauss-Markov conditions. And we estimate the above equation by the OLS estimator. Write down $b$ as a function of $y_{i}$'s. }
\item{Write down residuals by using $\be=\by-\bX b$ formula.}
\item{Calculate the average of the residuals. Explain why your answer makes sense or why it does not make sense.}
\end{enumerate}
